About Seals

Seals, scientifically known as pinnipeds, have 32 different types. They are carnivorous and are semiaquatic. Seal diet ranges, though they typically eat squid, molluscs, crustaceans (crabs), shellfish, and krill.

Leopard seals, however, eat penguins, seabirds, and other seals. While all seals have ears, some seals have visible ears. 16 species of seals have external ear flaps, six of which being furred. How cute!

Fun Fact

Seals can sleep both on land and in water! When underwater, seals sleep in a "bottling" position, so that they can automatically come up for air whenever needed.
